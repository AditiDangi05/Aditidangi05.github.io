B2B Content Strategy Blueprint: How I’d Go From Zero to Inbound Leads in 60 Days

The Premise

If I joined a B2B SaaS startup tomorrow with:

  • No content assets
  • No ads budget
  • No existing inbound traffic

Here’s exactly how I’d turn that into consistent inbound leads in just 60 days - as a fresher who plans like a pro.

Phase 1 - The “Laser Focus” Setup (Days 1–5)

Before creating anything, I’d:

  1. Pick one ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) - The biggest inbound killer is trying to talk to everyone.
  2. Find 3 pain points that keep them awake at 3 AM - Using LinkedIn stalking, industry forums, and direct outreach.
  3. Audit competitors’ top content - Identify gaps they’re not covering (or topics where they’re too generic).

Deliverable by Day 5: A “Pain Point Map” with 3 high-priority themes for content.

Phase 2 - Building the Lead Path (Days 6-15)

Here’s where most beginners fail - they start posting without thinking of the end goal. Instead, I’d map the content funnel:

  • Top of Funnel (TOFU): Short LinkedIn posts + industry insights → awareness.
  • Middle of Funnel (MOFU): Blog posts + problem-solution breakdowns → trust.
  • Bottom of Funnel (BOFU): Case study-style landing pages → conversion.

Example: If ICP = HR managers in SaaS companies →

  • TOFU post: “The 3 biggest onboarding mistakes remote teams make”
  • MOFU blog: “How to cut employee churn by 40% in 90 days”
  • BOFU page: “Our remote onboarding toolkit that saved $250K in turnover costs”

Phase 3 - Publishing with Purpose (Days 16-45)

Weekly output:

  • 3 LinkedIn posts (personal founder voice + industry hot takes)
  • 1 authority blog post (SEO + thought leadership)
  • 1 customer story snippet (mini-case study style)

My personal twist as a fresher: I’d repurpose everything - one blog post becomes 5 micro-content pieces for LinkedIn, a carousel, and an email newsletter snippet.

Phase 4 - Engagement → Leads (Days 46-60)

The inbound engine only works if you actively convert. Here’s my plan:

  • Reply to every comment within 2 hours.
  • DM people who engage meaningfully, offering a free resource (lead magnet).
  • Add CTAs to blog posts directing to a “Quick Discovery Call” page.

By Day 60, the goal is:

  • Consistent inbound DMs from ICPs.
  • An email list of early subscribers.
  • At least 1 booked sales call per week from organic content.

Why This Blueprint Belongs in My Portfolio

I’m not pretending I’ve run this for a client. I’m showing that even as a fresher, I think in systems, not just content pieces. This plan proves I:

  • Understand the B2B sales process.
  • Can map content to the funnel.
  • Focus on measurable results (leads, not just likes).
