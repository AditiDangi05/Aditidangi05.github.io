[Ghostwriting Concept] How I'd Turn a Founder's Struggle into a Lead-Magnet Post

The Scenario

I'm a fresher. No clients yet.

But I study what works - a lot.

And if a B2B SaaS founder came to me tomorrow saying:

"My LinkedIn posts get likes, but no leads"

Here's exactly how I'd craft one post to change that.

The Hypothetical Post (Script I'd Write)

The Night I Almost Shut Down My Startup

In 2021, my co-founder quit.

We had 2 months of runway left.

My inbox was full of investor rejections.

That night, I opened a Word doc and titled it "Resignation Letter."

But I didn't finish it.

Instead, I remembered a lesson from my first mentor:

"When sales are stuck, talk to your best customers, not your best friends."

I emailed 7 existing customers and asked:

"What problem would hurt most if we disappeared tomorrow?"

Every single one said the same thing.

We rebuilt our landing page around it.

3 weeks later: highest month of revenue yet.

If you're stuck right now: your next growth hack might be hidden in your last 5 customer calls.

What's the best customer insight you've ever uncovered?

Why I Wrote It This Way (The Psychology)

  1. Opens with high-stakes drama: human brain pays more attention to survival stories than stats.
  2. Shows vulnerability: founders connect with honesty more than polished "wins."
  3. Mentor wisdom: builds authority via a borrowed voice.
  4. One action clear result: makes the advice feel doable.
  5. Ends with a question: → turns readers into commenters, boosting reach.

How I'd Use This Post

If I were working with a founder, I'd:

  • Post this on their LinkedIn profile.
  • Reply to every comment within 1 hour (algorithm boost).
  • DM everyone who engages meaningfully, offering a free 15-min chat.
  • Track leads generated over 1 week.

Why This Belongs in My Portfolio (Even as a Fresher)

I'm not claiming I've done it.

I'm showing how I'd do it - with enough detail that a founder could try it today and get results.

This isn't just "I can write."

It's: "I can think like a strategist, plan for engagement, and write for conversions."
